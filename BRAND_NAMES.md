# Brand rename ideas

Naming directions that can support a **classy, cohesive** wordmark or symbol and read closer to a **wardrobe studio** than a generic “org” or tool.

---

## Refined / editorial

Strong fit for a minimal logo: thin serif, high-contrast sans, or a simple monogram.

| Name | Notes |
|------|--------|
| **Vestiary** | Latin-tinged, quiet luxury. Logo: thin serif or high-contrast sans + simple **V** monogram. |
| **Linen & Rail** | Physical closet metaphor (fabric + hanger rail). Logo: **ampersand** or a rail line under the word. |
| **Atelier Closet** | Fashion-studio mood. Logo: small **A** badge + clean sans. |
| **Capsule Studio** | Modern wardrobe minimalism. Logo: **circle or capsule** shape + geometric type. |

---

## Short, ownable

Good for app icon, tab bar, and word-of-mouth.

| Name | Notes |
|------|--------|
| **Vestis** | “Garment” (Latin root). Logo: abstract **folded cloth** or **V** notch. |
| **Ensemble** | Outfit-forward, approachable premium. Logo: **overlapping shapes** (e.g. two rectangles). |
| **Railhouse** | Rail + home for clothes. Logo: **horizontal bar** in the “R” or “H”. |
| **Sortée** | French flair / “sorted” association. Logo: elegant **É** in wordmark (mind spelling and pronunciation). |

---

## Calm, personal

Matches a soft, neutral UI (glass, slate shells, photo-first grids).

| Name | Notes |
|------|--------|
| **My Loom** | Fabric and care. Logo: **thread arc** or minimal loom mark. |
| **Still & Co.** | Curated closet, still-life mood. Logo: **frame** around “S” or a quiet **&**. |
| **Harbor Wardrobe** | “Safe harbor” for your clothes. Logo: **H** + subtle abstract **roof or shelter** line (keep minimal to stay classy). |

---

## Keeping “closet” in the name

Clear category + room for an elevated mark.

| Name | Notes |
|------|--------|
| **Closet Atelier** | Studio + closet; logo can pair hanger or rail with refined type. |
| **Closet Index** | Slightly more “product”; logo: **index tab** or notch on the “C”. |
| **The Curated Closet** | Magazine-like; **TCC** monogram for icon. |

---

## Logo and naming checklist

- Use **one hero typeface** across product surfaces; a second display face only on marketing if you need contrast.
- Favor names that allow a **simple monogram** (one or two letters) for the mobile app icon.
- Avoid loaded suffixes (**Org**, **Hub**, **Pro**, **AI**) in the **primary** name unless that is the core brand promise—they often read as tooling, not wardrobe.

---

## How to narrow

Pick a lane first:

- **Luxury / editorial** → Vestiary, Atelier Closet, Vestis  
- **Minimal tech** → Capsule Studio, Closet Index, Ensemble  
- **Warm / personal** → My Loom, Still & Co., Linen & Rail  

Then validate: domain, app store, trademark search, and how it sounds when someone says *“I’ll add it in [Name].”*
